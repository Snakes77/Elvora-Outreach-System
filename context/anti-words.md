# Anti-Words & Phrases — Elvora Consulting

## Banned Words
- Delve
- Utilise (use "use")
- Leverage (use "draw on")
- Synergy
- Robust (overused in care sector)
- Going forward
- Touch base
- Reach out (use "contact" or "get in touch")
- Streamline
- Holistic (unless quoting CQC)
- Best practice (use specific examples)
- World-class
- Game-changer
- Empower (show it, don't say it)
- Passionate (overused — show it through specifics)

## Banned Phrases
- "In today's ever-changing landscape"
- "It goes without saying"
- "At the end of the day"
- "We are committed to excellence"
- "Our team of experts"
- "Don't hesitate to contact us"
- "I hope this email finds you well"

## Tone Anti-Patterns
- Never condescending — audience are experienced professionals
- Never vague — always specific and actionable
- Never scaremonger about CQC — inform and prepare instead
- Never oversell — let outcomes and stats do the work

## Formatting Anti-Patterns
- No walls of text
- No excessive exclamation marks (max 1 per piece, ideally 0)
- No ALL CAPS for emphasis
- No pointless hyphens — only hyphenate when genuinely needed for clarity (e.g. "well-being" is correct; "care-home" is not)

## Language
- UK English spelling and grammar at all times
- Organise not organize, behaviour not behavior, colour not color, recognise not recognize, centre not center, practice (noun) / practise (verb)
- No Americanisms in vocabulary or phrasing
