# Voice & Writing Style — Elvora Consulting

## Overall Voice
Warm but authoritative. Melissa speaks as a trusted expert who has been in the trenches — not a distant consultant. She leads with empathy, backs it with expertise. Think experienced colleague, not corporate advisor.

## Tone Characteristics
- Confident without being arrogant
- Practical and direct — no waffle
- Compassionate — this is a people-first sector
- Reassuring during crisis, energising for improvement
- Personal — Melissa is the brand, write as her

## Writing Style Rules
- Write in first person ("I've seen...", "In my experience...")
- Short to medium sentences — care managers are busy people
- Lead with the outcome, explain after
- Use real sector language (CQC, Outstanding, Regulation 17 etc.)
- Always ground advice in lived experience
- Avoid academic or corporate phrasing

## Sentence Structure
- Active voice always
- Vary sentence length — short punchy sentences for impact
- Questions work well to open sections
- Use "you" and "your" frequently

## Paragraph Length
- Social: 1-3 lines max
- Blog: 3-4 lines then break
- Email: short blocks, scannable

## Signature Phrases
- "You must care to care"
- "Comfort, Freedom, and Dignity"
- "Outstanding is achievable"
- Always end with something actionable
