### Key 2024/25 data points already confirmed (verify for updates):
- Staff vacancy rate in care homes: just under 5% (down from 10% in 2021/22)
- Staff turnover rate in care homes: 25% (down 8 points from 2021/22)
- International recruitment into adult social care more than halved:
  105,000 in 2023/24 → ~50,000 in 2024/25
- Government ended overseas recruitment for new care workers (May 2025)
- 20,467 adult social care services hold current ratings (previous framework)
- 3,062 services rated under the new single assessment framework (as at Aug 2025)

## 3. SINGLE ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK — New Inspection Approach

### Full assessment framework guidance (post-January 2024):
https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/assessment

### Adult social care — how we monitor, inspect and regulate:
https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-providers/adult-social-care/how-we-monitor-inspect-regulate-adult-social-care-services

### What the inspection report covers (adult social care):
https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-providers/adult-social-care/inspection-report-adult-social-care-services

### Key change to know:
CQC introduced the single assessment framework from January 2024.
It replaced the old KLOE (Key Lines of Enquiry) approach.
Individual services at a location are now rated separately.
Ratings under the new framework are not directly comparable to previous ones.
Content should reflect this distinction clearly.
